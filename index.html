🪵 Woodoku Game Basics

Woodoku is a wood-themed grid puzzle that blends the classic block-fitting gameplay with Sudoku-style 3×3 region clearing. The board is a 9×9 grid divided into nine smaller 3×3 boxes, giving you three ways to clear and score.

How to Play

  • Each round gives you 3 wooden pieces of random shapes.
  • Drag a piece onto the 9×9 grid to place it in an open position.
  • You must place all 3 pieces before new ones appear.
  • Completing a full row, column, or 3×3 region clears it from the board.
  • Clear multiple lines or regions at once for a combo bonus.
  • The game ends when no remaining piece fits anywhere on the board.

Strategy Tips

  • Think about all three pieces before placing the first one.
  • Prioritise completing 3×3 regions — they free up concentrated space quickly.
  • Avoid building isolated clusters in corners where pieces get trapped.
  • Set up multi-clear combos by nearly completing several lines at once.

🧠 AI Puzzle Solver Tools

Grid puzzle games like Woodoku get progressively harder as the board fills up. What starts as easy placement quickly becomes a challenge where a single wrong move can leave you stuck with no room for your remaining pieces.

This is where AI-powered puzzle solvers can help. These tools analyze the current board state and available pieces, then evaluate thousands of possible placements to recommend the strongest move. Instead of guessing, you get data-backed suggestions that consider line clears, region completions, board openness, and future flexibility.

Solvers are especially useful when you find yourself stuck on hard levels where every placement feels risky and one misstep ends the run. Rather than restarting, a solver can identify opportunities you might have missed and help you develop better pattern recognition over time.

Whether you use them as a rescue tool or a learning aid, AI solvers are becoming a valuable companion for anyone serious about improving at grid-based puzzle games.
